Marker readiness panel

Confidence-tiered candidate markers for structural-haplotype genotyping in Clarias gariepinus broodstock breeding programmes.
Each promoted inversion candidate is assigned to one of four confidence tiers based on per-variant allele frequencies in three karyotype groups (STD/STD, STD/INV, INV/INV) and on the availability of positive/negative controls. Tier 1 is a private indel/SNP tag with clean dosage (AF_STD ≤ 0.02, AF_HET ∈ [0.25, 0.75], AF_INV ≥ 0.80) AND identified controls in all three groups AND bighead specificity confirmed. Tier 2 is a multi-marker panel (≥3 linked private markers) or a single strong tag with imperfect dosage. Tier 3 is a breakpoint PCR candidate awaiting wet-lab validation — demoted from prior architecture because breakpoint precision is uncertain. Tier 4 is exploratory only. Because the target breeding system involves interspecific F₁ hybrids, candidate markers are additionally screened against the bighead catfish orthologous genome to distinguish African inversion tags from maternal-species sequence differences. Candidate markers are provided as a starting resource and should be locally validated before routine breeding deployment.
