Statistical profile of inversion-associated genomic features

Comparative summary of breakpoint context, genomic composition, functional cargo, population variation, and breeding-relevant burden across parental catfish genomes.
To determine whether inversion candidates represent random structural variation or biologically distinct genomic units, this page quantifies their breakpoint context, repeat environment, gene cargo, population-genetic properties, and cross-species orthologous context. Each statistic is compared against chromosome- and size-matched genomic backgrounds where possible. Population-based tests are performed in the African catfish broodstock panel, whereas bighead catfish values represent orthologous genomic context unless population resequencing data are available.
