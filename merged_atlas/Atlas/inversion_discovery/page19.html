What "no detectable inversion" means — and what it does not
You can never prove no inversions in a region with 100% certainty. What this page shows is regions where no inversion signal was detected above the study’s resolution, sample size, and callability limits. Tiny inversions, very rare inversions (carried by 1–2 fish), inversions in repetitive or low-callability regions, and inversions whose breakpoints fall outside short-read mappability can all be missed. The region_status field is the right level of claim (e.g. no_detectable_inversion_high_confidence), not a binary positive / negative.

Evidence layers
multi-layer battery
Each region is evaluated against the following evidence layers. A high-confidence negative passes all of them; a low-power negative may have one or more layers in a "limited" state.

Upload format — negative_regions.json
region schema
One row per region. chr / start_bp / end_bp define the interval. region_status is one of the five values above. evidence is an object with one boolean (or "limited") per layer. notes and citation are optional.

Methods note
manuscript-ready
Suggested manuscript wording (Methods):
We classified regions as inversion-negative when no candidate was detected across all evidence layers (local PCA, GHSL, SV calls, heterozygosity-block, haplotype-divergence) given the study’s resolution of [X SNPs / window, N samples] and the available callable mask. Regions without inversion candidates were not interpreted as definitively inversion-free; rather, they were classified as showing no detectable polymorphic inversion signal under the resolution, sample size, and callability limits of our multi-layer framework.
Or shorter (Discussion):
Absence calls indicate no detectable inversion signal in our cohort, not proof of absolute inversion absence.
