The idea
This page mirrors page 1's pipeline (per-window analysis → window×window similarity → MDS → cluster → candidate intervals) but reads per-sample nucleotide diversity (θπ) instead of dosage. Two scrubbers, two independent input signals, same shape of output.
A region that hits BOTH scrubbers' candidate lists is ~certainly real biology — that's the orthogonal validation. A region that hits ONLY this scrubber is something the genotype scrubber can't see (sweeps, balancing selection, Het-class subdivisions, mis-assemblies) — that's where new biology lives.

Scale — locked to win50000.step10000
ANGSD thetaStat emits θπ at four scales (in 02_heterozygosity/03_theta/): win5000.step1000, win10000.step2000, win50000.step10000 (multiscale) and win500000.step500000 (main). The chromosome-wide scrubber locks to win50000.step10000 for three reasons:

Browser memory: sim_mat is n_windows². Page 1 already handles ~5 000 windows ≈ 100 M cells; doubling that crashes most browsers. Below 50 kb, the matrix exceeds 1 GB.
Per-window θπ stability: at 9× coverage with ~10–40 sites/kb of callable density, a 5 kb window has 50–200 callable sites — the per-sample θπ variance is large enough that local-PCA on those values mostly captures noise. At 50 kb you have ~500–2 000 sites per window; the per-sample estimates are stable enough to PCA meaningfully. Below ~30 kb you embed noise, not biology.
Why finer step doesn't buy you breakpoint resolution: page 1's breakpoints are sharp because the local-PCA clustering signal is binary (in/out of inversion). The θπ analog is a diversity gradient across the inversion's flanks, not a step. Even at 1 kb step, the θπ-PCA breakpoint is diffuse over 20–50 kb of genomic space — the underlying biological gradient sets the floor. The finer scales (5/10 kb) are still useful for per-candidate breakpoint inspection (planned, post-v4.0 boundary panel) but not for the chromosome-wide scrubber.
