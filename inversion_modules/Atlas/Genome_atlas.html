The third atlas
schema-only · phase A
The Inversion Atlas reads the chromosome JSON to find structural rearrangements; the Diversity Atlas reinterprets the same data through a population-genetic-diversity lens. The Genome Atlas is the third sibling: it grounds both in the actual reference assembly + its annotations.
Where the other two atlases ask "what is segregating in this cohort?", the Genome Atlas asks "what is the genome itself?" — assembly quality, chromosome architecture, gene content, repeat landscape, conserved synteny with related Siluriformes, and the variant annotations that let inversion candidates be interpreted in terms of disrupted genes or regulatory elements.
Three contexts the cohort lives in: this Genome Atlas (the reference) — Inversion Atlas (what's polymorphic in 226 samples) — Diversity Atlas (how diverse / inbred / structured the cohort is). The Genome Atlas is where you cross-reference an inversion to its gene cargo (phase 12) without leaving the tool.

Planned panels
phase B+
Assembly QC banner
BUSCO single-copy %, contig N50, scaffold N50, gap rate, T2T completeness chip per chromosome.
Chromosome strip
Length-scaled per-chromosome rows with stacked density tracks (gene / repeat / conserved). Click to drill.
Gene cargo overlay
For each promoted candidate in the Inversion Atlas, list the genes inside the inversion span. Highlight deleterious-variant overlap from VESM / SIFT4G / SnpEff.
TE / repeat panel
Per-class repeat density across the chromosome. Test breakpoint zones for repeat enrichment (a known mechanism for inversion fixation).
Synteny ribbon
Pairwise collinearity blocks vs related Siluriformes (e.g. C. macrocephalus, I. punctatus). Ancestral karyotype reconstruction from Cactus.
Conserved-element atlas
UCEs and phastCons-derived conserved elements as a sub-track. Used to score regulatory disruption when a conserved element falls in an inversion zone.
Variant-annotation overlay
SnpEff impact categories (HIGH / MODERATE / LOW / MODIFIER) summarized per inversion candidate.
Phasing roadmap
ship-when-ready
phase A

phase B
Chromosome overview renderer — one row per chrom, length-scaled, with gene-density + repeat-density tracks. Wires chromosome_map + gene_track + repeat_track layers.
phase C
Gene-cargo overlay on candidates — for each promoted inversion, list the genes inside its span. Surfaces deleterious-variant overlap when MODULE_CONSERVATION outputs land.
phase D
Synteny + ancestral karyotype — Cactus pairwise blocks rendered as a ribbon track. Cross-references the Inversion Atlas's σ-verdicts at chromosome scale.
phase E
Variant-annotation overlay — SnpEff/VEP impact categories per candidate. Final piece for the "what does this inversion break?" question.
